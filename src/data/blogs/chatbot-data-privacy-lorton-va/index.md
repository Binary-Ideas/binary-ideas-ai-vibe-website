## 6 Costly Chatbot Data Privacy Mistakes in Lorton VA

### 1. Skipping Data Encryption

Storing chat logs without encryption is like leaving your cash drawer open. Use chat platforms that encrypt data in transit and at rest, especially when conversations mention finances or health.

### 2. Collecting Too Much Information

If you don’t need birth dates, social security numbers, or detailed medical notes—don’t ask for them. The more you collect, the more you must protect and justify to regulators.

### 3. Ignoring Consent Requirements

Customers should know when a bot is collecting data and why. Add clear disclosures up front and obtain consent before storing personal details.

### 4. Failing to Monitor Third-Party Integrations

Many chatbots plug into CRMs, payment systems, or email tools. If those integrations are outdated or insecure, your entire setup is exposed. Audit connections regularly and disable ones you no longer need.

### 5. No Incident Response Plan

Breaches happen. Without a plan, you’ll scramble to notify customers, patch systems, and cooperate with authorities. Document who to call, how to isolate the bot, and how to log what happened.

### 6. Forgetting Human Training

Staff still copy transcripts, export data, or respond manually. Teach everyone how to handle sensitive info, spot phishing attempts, and escalate odd chatbot behavior.

## Extra Ways to Keep Your Chatbot Secure

- Update chatbot software and plugins promptly.
